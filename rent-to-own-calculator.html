Understanding Rent-to-Own

Rent-to-own (lease option) agreements let you rent a home with the option to buy it at a predetermined price after a set period. A portion of your rent may go toward the purchase.

Key Components

  • Option Fee: Upfront payment (1-5% of price) for the right to buy later
  • Rent Premium: Higher-than-market rent, with extra going toward purchase
  • Rent Credit: Portion of rent applied to down payment (15-30% typical)
  • Purchase Price: Often locked in at signing, can work for or against you

Risks to Consider

If you don't exercise the option, you lose the option fee and rent credits. Make sure you can qualify for a mortgage by the end of the lease period.
