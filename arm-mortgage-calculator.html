Understanding ARM Mortgages

An adjustable-rate mortgage (ARM) has an interest rate that changes periodically based on market conditions. ARMs typically offer lower initial rates than fixed-rate mortgages.

ARM Terminology

  • 5/1 ARM: Fixed rate for 5 years, then adjusts annually
  • Initial Rate: The starting interest rate during fixed period
  • Adjustment Period: How often the rate changes after initial period
  • Rate Caps: Limits on how much rate can change
  • Index: Benchmark rate (SOFR, Treasury) that ARM tracks
  • Margin: Percentage added to index for your rate

When ARMs Make Sense

  • Planning to sell before the fixed period ends
  • Expect income to increase significantly
  • Interest rates are expected to decrease
  • Want lower initial payments for cash flow
