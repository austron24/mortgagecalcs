Important: Balloon Payment Risk

When the balloon payment is due, you must either pay the remaining balance in full, refinance the loan, or sell the property. Make sure you have a plan for this payment before taking out a balloon mortgage.

What is a Balloon Mortgage?

A balloon mortgage is a loan where you make regular monthly payments based on a longer amortization schedule, but the entire remaining balance becomes due as a single large "balloon" payment after a shorter term.

Common Balloon Terms

  • 5/25 Balloon: 5-year term with 25-year amortization
  • 7/23 Balloon: 7-year term with 23-year amortization
  • 10/20 Balloon: 10-year term with 20-year amortization

Pros and Cons

Advantages: Lower initial payments, potentially lower interest rates, good for short-term ownership

Risks: Large payment due at end, refinancing risk if rates rise or credit changes, potential for losing the property
