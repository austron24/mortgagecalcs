What is Earnest Money?

Earnest money (also called a good faith deposit) is a deposit made to demonstrate you're serious about buying the home. It's held in escrow and applied to your down payment or closing costs.

Typical Amounts

  • 1-2%: Common in buyer's markets or slower markets
  • 2-3%: Standard in balanced markets
  • 3-5%+: Competitive offers in seller's markets

Getting Your Deposit Back

You typically get your earnest money back if you cancel due to contingencies (inspection, financing, appraisal). You may lose it if you back out without a valid contingency reason.
