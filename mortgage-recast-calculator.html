What is Mortgage Recasting?

Recasting (or re-amortizing) is when you make a large lump sum payment to reduce your principal, then the lender recalculates your monthly payment based on the new, lower balance while keeping your original term and rate.

When Recasting Makes Sense

  • You have cash from home sale, inheritance, or bonus
  • Your current rate is lower than today's rates
  • You want lower payments without refinancing costs
  • You have good credit but don't want another inquiry

Recast Requirements

Most lenders require a minimum lump sum ($5,000-$10,000+), charge a small fee ($150-500), and only allow recasting on conventional loans (not FHA/VA). Check with your servicer for specific requirements.
