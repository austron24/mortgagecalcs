Understanding Escrow Accounts

An escrow account is a holding account where your lender collects monthly payments for property taxes, homeowners insurance, and sometimes PMI. This ensures these bills are paid on time.

What's Included in Escrow?

  • Property Taxes: Paid 1-2 times per year to your local government
  • Homeowners Insurance: Annual premium divided into monthly payments
  • PMI: If your down payment is less than 20%
  • Flood Insurance: Required in flood zones

Escrow Cushion

Lenders may require a cushion of 2 months' escrow payments to cover potential increases in taxes or insurance.
